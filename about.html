My Tumor Story


In November of 2021, I was diagnosed with a Giant Cell Tumor in my right leg. I had been experiencing some serious pain in my right knee for several months, but attributed the pain to be a result of stress due to marathon training throughout the first half of 2021. But, after a particularly painful run in early November, where I attempted to walk without a limp that I had developed and was unable to do so, I decided to go to the doctor, assuming an ACL injury. Well instead I was greeted with a Cancer scare... so much fun! Turns out I was fortunate and found myself with a Giant Cell Tumor, which is a benign tumor that is not cancerous, but extremely agressive. I was told that had I waited much longer I would have lost my entire leg.

I spend late November through Febuary completely unable to bear weight, having received a couple surgeries, resulting in a plate and 9 screws in my right leg, and a pretty narly scar. I was unable to walk, and spent most of my time in bed. Througout this whole period I refused to take a semester off of school, being driven to class by the Unviersity Paratransit Service. This period was mentally straining and tough for me, I felt alone and isolated after being the most social I had been my entire life. I am grateful for the friends and family that supported me through this time, without them I would have been lost.

Well, even after weight bearing began again, in April my tumor came back, albiet much smaller. I was told that surgery was not a viable option again, but we could try a medication with high success rates. I was put on Denosumab, a medication that is used to treat osteoporosis, but has been shown to be effective in treating Giant Cell Tumors. I am currently a year out from my reoccurance and all is looking well as of my last scan. In fact I have began running again (the look on my oncologist's face when I told her that was priceless). I am still on Denosumab, and will be indefinetly, which is not without side effects, including fairly constant bone aches throuhout my body, which I struggle with but keep quite about.

My reason in sharing this is not for sympathy, but to show that with a support system, a relentless attitude, and some luck, you can get through anything life throws at you. However, I benefitted greatly from my support system, and I know others must be dealing with similar issues without the same support system. So, if you are reading this and are dealing with chronic illness, a massive tumor, or any other health issue, please reach out to me. I would love to talk and help in any way.
